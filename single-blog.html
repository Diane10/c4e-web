Intore Dancing

Intore Dancing Dancing might sound to many as more of an entertainment activity, but it goes beyond just the joy of body movement. From the ancient times, dances have been very valuable aspects in identifying different core cultures of societies. Narrowing down to Africa, and Rwanda in particular, there are different kinds of dancing that are attached to specific culture practices. One of the popular Rwandan dances is known as Intore dancing. The finest displays of Rwanda’s dynamic traditional musical and dance styles are performed by the Intore Dance Troupes. Founded several centuries ago, the Intore, (The Chosen Ones) who performed exclusively for the Royal Court, were given military training and taught the technique of jumping which forms a significant part of the dance. Performed wearing grass wigs and clutching spears this dance is a true spectacle of Rwanda. Live dance performances can be seen at cultural villages, museums and as entertainment at many lodges and hotels across Rwanda. The Gorilla Guardians village in Musanze, and the National Museum of Rwanda have regular performances. (Visit Rwanda, 2020)

C4E stands ready to ensure that the core culture that drives different practices including but not limited to music and dance are recognized. At C4E we believe that “entertainment is influential and emotional. It is therefore important to keep at heart our cultural values that drive music, because forgetting them means forgetting our natural joy.” Music and dance are an integral part of Rwandan ceremonies, festivals, social gatherings, and storytelling. The most famous traditional dance is Intore, a highly choreographed routine consisting of three components - the ballet, performed by women; the dance of heroes, performed by men, and the drums. Traditionally, music is transmitted orally with styles varying between the social groups. Drums are of great importance, the royal drummers having enjoyed high status within the court of the mwami. Drummers usually play together in groups of seven or nine. Wikipedia,2020.

Intore: The longest-living traditional Rwandan dance reserved for elite royal soldiers The Intore dancers were an army trained to go to war and defend the king against internal attacks. These warriors were selected at a very early age and taken into the royal court to receive training. Families often prayed for the blessing of many boys so that one of them could be chosen as a warrior of the king. The selection brought prestige and honour to the family.

Our Culture is rich and enjoyable. Rwandans, especially the youth must be at the center of owning and preserving this great treasure, since it is one of the ways we get to portray the real picture of who we are. “Culture is a unique value proposition of a society, losing it means losing.”C4E quote. It true that many people who have gotten a chance to see the Rwandan traditional dance of Intore being performed, have wondered why they put on such an attire, use spears, shields and horns. Well lets shade some light on this. It should be now clear that in the culture of Rwanda, Intore was a team of worriors that were selected, and educated to serve the king. They knew much about the country and were equipped with battle skills which were necessary for fighting for the nation. Every time they returned from the battle field, they now performed the Intore dance as a sign of victory.

Although this dance is now performed in various kinds of ceremonies by civilians, it must be boldly understood that the Intore cultural dance was/and is still a sign of victory, and it is a dance of the warriors together with the music in the company.
